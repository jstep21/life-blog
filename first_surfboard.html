Chasing Waves: The Story of My First Surfboard

From renting boards to owning my first, this is the journey of how I found my perfect wave companion on the shores of Florida.

June 12th, 2017 Jake Stephens

Living in Tampa, Florida, meant that my local beaches were on the Gulf of Mexico, where the waves were often gentle and flat. But my passion for surfing demanded more than the Gulf could offer. So, every chance I got, I would wake up before dawn, usually around 6 or 7 in the morning, and make the drive to the East Coast. The Atlantic Ocean promised bigger, better waves, and I found myself regularly heading to spots like Sebastian Inlet, Melbourne Beach, Cocoa Beach, and New Smyrna Beach, always in search of the perfect swell.

For months, I rented boards from a local surf shop, content to borrow what I needed. But as my trips became more frequent, and my desire to improve grew stronger, I knew it was time to invest in a board of my own. Owning a surfboard felt like a rite of passage, a sign that I was truly committed to the sport.

One day, after an early morning session at Sebastian Inlet, I wandered into a nearby surf shop. I struck up a conversation with one of the workers, who was passionate about the boards they crafted in-house. He talked me through the different shapes, sizes, and materials, each designed for specific conditions and skill levels. After hearing his insights and considering my own experience, I decided on a 6'10'' board—a perfect blend of stability and maneuverability.

That first ride on my new board felt like nothing I'd ever experienced. The quality and fit were just right, and it wasn't long before I noticed how much my surfing improved. I could paddle faster, catch waves more easily, and ride with a newfound confidence. The board became an extension of myself, and suddenly, I was no longer just a weekend surfer—I was a surfer, period.

Surfing isn't just about the thrill of catching a wave; it's about the early mornings, the long drives, and the community you find along the way. It's the quiet moments sitting on your board, waiting for the next set, the feeling of being in tune with the ocean. And of course, it's about finding that one board that changes everything. For me, that 6'10'' board did just that, turning my love for surfing into something deeper, more personal, and more profound.
